Through pain, experience becomes real. 
2
What is uncertain becomes defined. 
3
What is passing becomes permanent. 
4
What is neutral becomes meaningful in a heavy, unavoidable way. 
5
Pain does not just accompany experience.
6
It constructs the sense that there is a real world being lived in.
7
Without pain, there is nothing holding experience into place.
8
What was taken as a stable reality begins to lose its structure, 
9
because that structure was never separate from the suffering that formed it. 
10
The solidity of life is not inherent; 
11
it is maintained by the pressure that gives it weight.
12
From this perspective, pain is not evidence of reality, 
13
but the mechanism that makes reality feel real. 
14
When it is absent, what remains is not a hidden deeper world,
15
but the fading of the illusion that anything was ever fixed to begin with.
